Persona 01 / 03
Renate Hoffmann
Retired Teacher
  • Age: 67
  • Location: Freiburg
  • Home: Owner, 1978 semi-detached
  • Tech: Moderate
  • Income: Fixed pension
"I just want to widen my bathroom door. My neighbour said I need an architect. For a door?"
Daily Workflow

Renate wakes at 7am, reads the newspaper with coffee, tends her garden, and cooks lunch for herself and sometimes her daughter. She checks email twice a day and uses WhatsApp to stay in touch with family. Her afternoons are spent at a Volkshochschule watercolour class or walking her dog, Bruno. She manages her finances carefully in a paper ledger. Any "admin" task — a phone call to an office, a form to fill — takes her significant emotional energy to initiate.

Psychological Drivers
Independence Not being a burden Pride in her home Fear of being cheated
Decision-Making Process

Renate asks her daughter first, then a neighbour. She distrusts anything that feels "too easy" — if the answer comes too fast she assumes something is wrong. She will read the same document three times before acting. She needs to feel she understands why a rule exists, not just what it is. A human she trusts is worth more than any app, but she's open to tools if her daughter vouches for them.

Biggest Frustrations

The Bauamt told her she'd need to submit a formal Bauantrag for structural changes — she doesn't know if widening a door counts. The architect she called quoted €800 "just for the consultation." She found a PDF from the Landratsamt but it was 47 pages. She closed it. She feels the system is not built for her, and she's right.

Secret Motivation

Her husband passed two years ago. The house modifications are about staying in the home they built together — not just for accessibility, but because leaving would feel like losing him a second time. She will not say this to anyone, including her daughter.

Persona 02 / 03
Tobias Bergmann
Logistics Operations Manager
  • Age: 41
  • Location: Hannover
  • Home: Owner, 2003 Reihenhaus
  • Tech: High
  • Income: €72k / year
"I've got twins on the way. I need to know if I can build an Anbau in the next 18 months. Not the theory — the actual answer."
Daily Workflow

Tobias starts work at 7:30, managing a team of 14 across two distribution hubs via Teams and their internal TMS. He solves problems under time pressure all day — missed deliveries, driver disputes, system outages. He's home by 6:30, cooks with his wife Lena, and has maybe 90 minutes in the evening before he's too tired to focus. He uses apps for everything: banking, grocery delivery, car maintenance reminders. He genuinely enjoys research but has no patience for friction.

Psychological Drivers
Control over outcomes Efficiency Providing for family Distrust of professionals who gatekeep
Decision-Making Process

Tobias is a systematic researcher. He'll spend two evenings reading forums, comparing quotes, building a spreadsheet. He wants data before he talks to anyone — showing up to a professional meeting uninformed feels like a failure. Once he has enough signal, he decides fast and doesn't second-guess. He tests tools on real problems immediately; if it doesn't give him something actionable in ten minutes, he abandons it.

Biggest Frustrations

He got three conflicting answers from three different architects about whether his Bebauungsplan allows a rear extension. One said definitely yes, one said "it depends," one said he'd need to commission a full survey first. He's spent €400 on preliminary consultations and knows less than when he started. He's furious that a process this important is this opaque, and he's vocal about it in online communities for homeowners.

Secret Motivation

Tobias grew up in a cramped flat in Dortmund. His parents rented their whole lives and never owned anything. This house — and the extension — is proof that he did something different. He needs it to work not just practically but symbolically, and he'd never admit how much that weighs on him.

Persona 03 / 03
Miriam Osei-Bonsu
Freelance Graphic Designer
  • Age: 34
  • Location: Leipzig
  • Home: Owner, 1930s Altbau flat
  • Tech: Very high
  • Income: Variable, avg. €48k
"I want to convert my spare room into a proper studio — maybe rent it to clients for shoots. But the WEG, the Finanzamt, the Bauamt — I don't know which world this problem even lives in."
Daily Workflow

Miriam works from home on brand and packaging projects, usually from 10am to 7pm with long creative stretches interrupted by client calls. She manages her own invoicing, taxes, and contracts — she's used to navigating bureaucracy alone but it costs her time she resents losing. She's active on Instagram, reads design blogs obsessively, and discovers new tools through her freelancer Slack community. She adopts early and recommends loudly when something works.

Psychological Drivers
Creative autonomy Financial stability Being taken seriously Anxiety about "doing it wrong"
Decision-Making Process

Miriam maps problems visually — she's drawn an actual diagram of who governs what for her flat renovation question. She's comfortable with ambiguity in creative work but finds legal ambiguity genuinely destabilising. She will wait longer than is rational for certainty before spending money. She checks in with two or three trusted freelancer friends before hiring any professional, and she will publicly review services (positively and negatively) on relevant forums.

Biggest Frustrations

Her Wohnungseigentümergemeinschaft has a 70-page Teilungserklärung she can barely parse. She's not sure if her planned changes are a bauliche Veränderung requiring WEG approval, a purely cosmetic change, or something that touches building law as well. She called her Hausverwaltung and was told to "consult a lawyer." The lawyer quoted €300/hr. She earns €80/hr. The math stings.

Secret Motivation

Buying the flat was the first major thing Miriam did that her parents — who immigrated from Ghana and rented all their lives in Germany — saw as proof of arrival. She wants to make it into something remarkable, partly for herself and partly as a monument to what they built. She treats every renovation setback as a small defeat she can't explain to them.

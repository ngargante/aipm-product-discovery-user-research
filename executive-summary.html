RechtImmobilien — Research Document
User Research: Executive Summary
Synthetic interviews · 3 personas
Prototype: RechtImmobilien v1
March 2026
Core finding
The pain users feel is not the cost of getting legal advice — it is the absence of anyone willing to give a straight answer and stand behind it. RechtImmobilien directly addresses this. The product's credibility, not its feature set, is what will determine adoption.
Key findings from user interviews
Strongest signal
Legal citations land across all three personas
The Legal Sources panel — specific §§ with per-source confidence scores — was the single most consistent positive reaction. Renate found citations reassuring without reading them. Tobias saw them as the tool to walk into architect meetings as an equal. Miriam used them as evidence of legal grounding for generated documents. This feature works differently for each user, but it works for all of them.
Conversion blocker
The accountability gap blocks engagement before the first question
No persona saw a named professional or clear liability statement. Renate asked "who is responsible if it's wrong?" before typing anything. Miriam wouldn't use the document generator without proof a Rechtsanwalt reviewed the template. For cautious users, this is not a post-use concern — it stops them from engaging at all.
Usability gap
Geographic scope is invisible — and users notice
Both Renate (Freiburg) and Tobias (Hannover) asked: is this answer for all of Germany or just for Berlin? The chat placeholder example — "Can I rent out my apartment in Berlin short-term?" — signals Berlin-only scope to users outside the capital. A simple copy fix could resolve one of the most common friction points.
Feature gap
Users don't know which law governs their question
Miriam had literally drawn a diagram — WEG, Baurecht, Steuerrecht — trying to map which authority her problem belonged to. The same confusion appeared implicitly in Tobias's Bebauungsplan question. Without a jurisdiction classifier upfront, users may get technically correct answers that still feel unresolved.
Opportunity
The Permit Wizard is the right entry point for action-oriented users
Tobias navigated to the Permit Wizard within 20 seconds. The step-by-step checklist matched the process map he had been building manually on a spreadsheet. The gap: the wizard is generic, not address-specific. He couldn't verify feasibility for his actual plot in Hannover.
Opportunity
Confidence scores need a "why" — not just a number
Tobias appreciated per-citation scores over a single answer-level score, but immediately asked: is a low score because the law is ambiguous, or because the AI is missing my local data? These require completely different responses from the user. A tooltip explaining the source of uncertainty would close this loop.

Recommendations
Do now
Prototype changes this week — no backend work required
Move citations above the fold
The Legal Sources panel is currently below a long answer body. Users who don't scroll will never see it — and it's the strongest conversion feature we have. Move it to a sticky sidebar or directly below the answer summary.
Add trust copy above the chat input
One line: "Answers backed by licensed Rechtsanwälte and specific §§ citations." This directly addresses the pre-engagement accountability gap that Renate and Miriam both flagged — and it's a copy change, not a product change.
Fix the Berlin placeholder example
Replace "Can I rent out my apartment in Berlin short-term?" with a location-neutral example, or add a Bundesland prompt. Users outside Berlin read the current placeholder as "this is for Berlin, not me."
Add a print / export button to answers
Both Renate and Tobias want to bring the answer to a trusted person (daughter, architect) or file it with their house documents. A browser print button or PDF export costs minimal dev time and directly enables the referral loop.
Plan and build
Requires design and development — prioritise by archetype value
Pre-chat jurisdiction triage
Two questions before the chat opens: "What type of property do you own?" and "What are you trying to do?" Routes the question, sets AI context, and — critically — tells users which legal domain their problem lives in before they type. Miriam named this as the missing feature most likely to make her pay.
Address-specific data integration
Enter your address, the system pulls your Bebauungsplan, GRZ and GFZ values, and setback requirements. This is the feature that converts the Permit Wizard from theoretical to actionable for Tobias-type users. Complex to build but defines whether the product can serve power users at all.
Municipal wait time layer
Add current Bauamt processing times per city to the Permit Wizard step estimates. Tobias noted the current "2–3 months" estimate is from 2019; Hannover is running 9–11 months. Real data here is a major differentiator over static guides.
Discuss as a team
Open questions requiring product and legal alignment
What does a low confidence score actually mean?
A score of 2/5 on a citation could mean the law itself is ambiguous (user should consult a specialist) or that the AI is missing local data (user should provide their Bebauungsplan and re-run). These require completely different actions. The product needs to surface the cause of uncertainty, not just the number. How do we classify and communicate this?
Who is the accountable human — and what is our liability position?
Users across all three segments asked for a named professional behind the answers. A real named Rechtsanwalt carries legal and liability implications. A "reviewed by legal professionals" badge is softer. What is our actual relationship with legal oversight? This decision shapes trust architecture across the whole product.
Is the Renate segment actually in our ICP?
Renate (60+, moderate tech, fixed pension) has the highest conversion friction and is unlikely to find us organically. She may be reachable only through referral from her trusted circle — a different acquisition strategy entirely. Do we design for her now, or treat her as a later segment reached via Miriam and Tobias's networks?
